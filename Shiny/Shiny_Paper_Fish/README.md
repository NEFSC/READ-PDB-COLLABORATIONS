# Shiny Paper Fish

We wrote this app with a little help from Gemini to allow users to explore the impact of paper fish (projected recruits which do not yet exist) on catch and SSB projections.

The basic idea is there is a population in equilibrium in 2025 that is controlled by an F value along with a constant M of 0.2. Projections are made from this equilibrium starting point based on a new recruitment (Projection R) and F value (Projection F). Projection recruitment is defined as a ratio to the equilibrium recruitment in 2025. The sliders for maturity A50, selectivity A50, and von B growth parameter K show how fishery and biology can impact the relative amount of paper fish in the projections.

The paper fish are shown in gray in the top plot while the biology and fishery characteristics are shown in the bottom plot. The user can view Catch or SSB in either total amount or proportions when examining the paper fish.

The red button returns all settings to their defaults. The blue button adds a line showing the paper fish amount or proportion over time when the button is pushed to allow easy comparison as sliders are moved.
